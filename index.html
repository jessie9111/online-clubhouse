Meet Faith!

Faith just left a school assembly in which the principal told all of the students that tomorrow was their last day at school. The governor of the state had just announced that all K-12 schools were closed for the rest of the school year, and Faith and her parents were so sad to hear this news. Faith loved her best friends Isabella and Rose, and she knew that she would definitely miss in-person math class with Ms. Johnson. 

Later that night at home, Faith and her parents were watching the news. “This situation is just so unpredictable, how do we even keep up with everything that’s going on with COVID-19? There’s so much media out there, Trump’s saying one thing while health officials are saying another, it’s overwhelming!” says Faith’s mom. 

Earlier that week, Ms. Johnson was talking about big data and how data science and data visualizations were so important in shaping people’s perceptions of the world. By analyzing large datasets, people can share observations to support their perspectives in a way that might not be entirely accurate. Faith wanted to make sure that her and her family were observing data based on accurate measurements. How can she use data science skills to explore and visualize data regarding COVID-19?
